Programs

Art and technology partnerships represent a unique and impactful approach to emerging technology research. To empower investigations and develop projects at this intersection, ThoughtWorks Arts runs a number of programs incubating artist and technologist collaborations.

Our work at ThoughtWorks Arts enables exploration into the impacts of emerging technologies on industry, culture and society. This includes programs such as Hardware Hack Lab, Art-A-Hack and the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency, each with a particular method, focus and outcomes.

{% include image.html file='/images/programs/thoughtworks-arts.png' alt='ThoughtWorks Arts includes programs such as Art-A-Hack, Hardware Hack Lab and the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency' class='no-border' %}

Together these programs cover a wide spectrum of modes of production, mechanisms of support, and approaches to inclusion. They range from the open-access community engagement of Hardware Hack Lab, through the facilitated cross-disciplinary collaboration of Art-A-Hack, and into the deep, focused cutting-edge research of the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency.

Each program is driven by the central insight that art provides a powerful lens for engaging with technology, particularly given the high degree of freedom within arts practice to make large conceptual leaps across technical, industrial and societal domains.

Hardware Hack Lab

Started in 2013, the Hardware Hack Lab has become a staple of the New York City creative technology scene. The lab is known as a free, weekly co-working community, offering access to high-end hardware and a community with a culture of exploration.

{% include image.html file='/images/programs/hardware-hack-lab.jpg' alt='The Hardware Hack Lab is a free space for artists, hackers and innovators every week in New York City' %}

Members are encouraged to bring technology they are working on and to exchange and share with others. The lab is often visited by guests from academia, industry, technology and art, and maintains an accessible supply of VR/AR/MR, electronics equipment and devices, providing food and enabling social interaction.
