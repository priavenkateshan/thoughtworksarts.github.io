Programs

Art and technology partnerships represent a surprisingly unique and impactful approach to emerging technology research. To empower investigations and develop projects at this intersection, ThoughtWorks Arts runs a number of programs incubating artist and technologist collaborations.

The programs of ThoughtWorks Arts enable investigations into the impacts of emerging technologies on industry, culture and society. Our programs include Hardware Hack Lab, Art-A-Hack and the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency, each with a unique focus, approach and outcomes.

{% include image.html file='/images/programs.png' alt='ThoughtWorks Arts includes programs such as Art-A-Hack, Hardware Hack Lab and the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency' class='no-border' %}

Together these programs cover a wide spectrum of modes of support, methods of inclusion, and nature of outcomes. They range from the open-access community support of Hardware Hack Lab, through the facilitated cross-disciplinary collaboration of Art-A-Hack, and into the deep, focused cutting edge issue research of the ThoughtWorks Arts Residency.

Each program is driven by the central insight that art provides a powerful lens for engaging with technology, in particular because of its high degree of freedom in making large conceptual leaps across technical, industrial and societal domains, delivering often unexpected outcomes.

How it works

Artists are invited spend 16 weeks in residence in New York, at ThoughtWorks, a global technology consultancy. During their stay, artists receive a regular stipend and project production support from a global network of analysts, designers, developers and other experts in the company.

ThoughtWorks runs the residency out of an desire to openly experiment, collaborate and share ideas with those asking hard questions about emerging technologies. Therefore, artists own all physical and intellectual property generated during their stay.
